"The Queen of Beaches"

Calangute Beach

Situated 15 km from Panji, Calangute Beach is the longest beach in North Goa, stretching from Candolim to Baga. Due to its sheer size and popularity, it is a hub for tourists and backpackers from all over the world.

The "Queen of Beaches", the Calangute Beach of Goa is among the top ten bathing beaches in the world.

Being one of the busiest and most commercial beaches of Goa, it is swarming with eating joints, shacks and clubs serving cocktails, beer and seafood.

The Calangute Beach is also known for its water sports activities like parasailing, water surfing, banana ride and jet-skiing.

It keeps you well-connected to the other beaches in the north such as Baga, Anjuna, Candolim,Aguada and many more.

"A Fortress of Tranquility"

Fort Agauda Tourism

Fort Aguada is a 17th-century Portuguese fort looking out at the confluence of Mondovi River and the Arabian Sea.

The crumbling ramparts of the fort stand on the Sinquerim Beach,approximately 18 km from Panji.

The highlight of the fort is a lone four-Storey lighthouse (which is one-of-its-kind in Asia) and a stunning view of the sunset.

The fort is so named after the Portuguese Word for water'Agua' and used to be a replenishing source of freshwater for sailors.

In fact, it has the capacity to hold 2,376,000 gallons of water and was one of the biggest freshwater reservoirs of Asia Fort Aguada has also been used as Aguada jail.

11111,A statue in front of the jail salutes the freedom fighters, giving the fort a touch of patriotism.
